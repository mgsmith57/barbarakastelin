Barbara was born in Switzerland. At school, her friends often begged her to tell them a funny story, one with exaggerations. Imagination carried Barbara along, as on a raft, through German, French and Russian language, their philosophies and literatures, as they were her familyâ€™s origins. Often she reverted to drawing and sketching people.

She studied French literature and philosophy at university, worked for the UN in Geneva and New York, and then in advertising. It was not a surprise that she should marry a British diplomat with whom she could travel to even more countries and revel in encounters with new cultures and interesting people.

At seventy now, she lives mostly in her Cambridge home and enjoys the magic her grandchildren make her part of. It became time to learn how to paint in oil, and a necessity to write novels in the hope that others might enjoy her exuberant stories.
